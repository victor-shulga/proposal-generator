# Call-deck framework — Viktor Shulga's high-ticket proposal structure

Source: Viktor's own carousel "Як я структурую пропоузал, коли продаю послуги на високі чеки"
(`Downloads/proposal structure.pdf`). This is the canonical order for the CALL DECK (variant A).
Render as a vertical scroll-snap deck, ~10 slides, big type, light copy, arrow-key nav.

## The order (TL;DR)
Why us → Challenge → Alternatives → Solution → Pricing (2-3) → Expected results →
How we work → Relevant cases → (Next step / close).

## Slide-by-slide + the principle behind each

1. **Cover** — wordmark, project, "prepared for [Contact/Company]", presenter, date.

2. **Why us → for THEM** (replaces "About company"). 
   *Principle: buyers care about THEIR business, problems, team. Opening with a "how awesome
   we are" slide is a bad idea.* Reframe to "why they should give you their money":
   - **ICP** — who wins from this service
   - **Use case** — how exactly you'll do it
   - **Benefit** — what the ICP gets
   → Move trust-flex stats (300+/95%) OUT of the opener; they belong on the cases slide.

3. **The Challenge** (replaces "Our expertise"). 
   *Principle: the reader cares about THEIR specific problem, not your full skill list. If you
   can frame their problem better than they can, it signals you have the best solution.*
   - Articulate the problem sharply.
   - **Add a buyer quote** — the lead's own words about the problem (screenshot their LinkedIn /
     paste from the call). Placeholder + note to insert the real quote.

4. **Alternatives** (replaces an "ROI" slide). 
   *Principle: presenting ROI at any sales-cycle stage correlates with ~27% LOWER close rate —
   buyers feel you're overreaching between what the service does and the money you wave. And
   buyers ALWAYS weigh alternatives — so show why you're the best option.* 
   Compare the real alternatives (in-house hire / freelancers / cheap offshore) vs the client,
   with the catch for each. No financial projections.

5. **Solution — how you deliver** (slides 4-10 in the source). 
   *Principle: if you present only scope, you lose the deal. Show HOW you deliver.* 
   Use the client's own signature method (e.g. their N-step delivery framework). Detail, not
   just a scope list.

6. **Pricing — 2-3 options** (slides 11-14). 
   *Principle: a single option makes the lead treat competitors as the alternative. Give 2-3.
   If trust isn't there yet, include a lower-involvement, lower-price entry option.* 
   Each tier carries **scope + timeline + expected outcome**. Anchor the middle ("Most chosen").
   Add the **PDF cost-breakdown button** here.

7. **Expected results** (slide 15) — the stages you go through and how long each takes
   (a timeline), plus the operational outcomes. NOT a financial ROI claim.

8. **How we work** (slide 16) — 3-4 simple steps so it's effortless to work with you.

9. **Relevant cases** (slides 17-20) — exact numbers. For trust, match the buyer's industry,
   company size and service. 2 cases is enough on a deck.

10. **Next step** — one clear CTA + contact. Close on the action, not on "thanks".

## Do / Don't
- ✗ Don't open with "About us" / a stats-flex. ✓ Open with why it matters for THEM.
- ✗ Don't show an ROI / payback-projection slide. ✓ Show alternatives + operational outcomes.
- ✗ Don't give one price. ✓ Give 2-3, each with scope+timeline+outcome.
- ✗ Don't present scope alone. ✓ Show the delivery method.
- ✓ Put the buyer's own problem-quote on the Challenge slide.
